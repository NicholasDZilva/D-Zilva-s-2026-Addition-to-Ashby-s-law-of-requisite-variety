# D'Zilva 2026 Addition to Ashby's Law of Requisite Variety

Building on Ashby’s Law of Requisite Variety:

**Ashby (1956):**
> "Only variety can absorb variety."

**D'Zilva Addition (2026):**
> "Only systems whose corrective capacity outpaces both disturbance and suppression can maintain structural integrity over time."

**Conceptual Formula:**
DI_effective = (DI_raw / C) + (suppression / 100)
Where:
- **DI_raw** = raw systemic distortion  
- **C** = corrective capacity of the system  
- **suppression** = proportion of independent signals suppressed  

**Context:**  
In modern AI, governance, and complex systems, ignoring invisible inefficiencies guarantees structural collapse. This addition highlights that maintaining systemic integrity requires active corrective capacity that outpaces disturbances and suppression, not just reactive speed.
